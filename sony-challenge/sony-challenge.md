# Sony Challenge
[Link](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1LDu9R9QbW2GS2DSzUHmxKXi80OB9Z5_ImSg47TjB5HM/edit?tab=t.0)
*Prompt:* What does the future of personal listening and creative empowerment look like — from a software and experience design perspective — for both artists and listeners?

You are not designing a new “device” or embedding new DSP-level features.
Instead, think about the ecosystem — how platforms, applications, and creative tools could work with hardware to unlock new forms of musical expression and connection.

****

**Requirements:**
1. Build / iterate on past Sony experiments (for example in the past Sony collaborated with Sony to build out a custom EQ with Olivia Rodrigo) — evolving how artists’ creative identity or sound can be experienced.

2. Focus on software layer, experience design, creative ecosystem, *not* headphone design

3. Explore how creative technologies such as source separation, AI-assisted remixing, adaptive sound rendering can contribute to a more interactive music experience valued by both creators and fans

4. Bridge pro and consumer worlds:
    - For artists/creators: offer new ways to express sonic identity, share intent, and connect emotionally with audiences.
    - For listeners: make them feel closer to the art and artist — maybe even part of the creative process itself.

5. Reflect what creators value most:
    - Authenticity — the music feels true to their sound.
    - Control — they can shape how their work is heard and experienced.
    - Connection — listeners experience their intent more deeply.
    - Playfulness — new ways for audiences to explore or reinterpret sound creatively.

6. Stay future-forward –  think hybrid experiences that live between software, hardware, and culture, shaping how music is created, discovered, and shared.
